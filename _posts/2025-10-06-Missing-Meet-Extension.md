## Traditions first

For the last 5 years, I have worked primarily remotely. For the last 3 (almost there), I’ve been working at [Contentsquare](https://contentsquare.com/) with headquarters in Paris. There are some coworkers in Berlin, but my main team fellows work from different cities: Paris, Lyon, Nice, Orléans, and Pula. 

It happens that a few times per year, we meet for an offsite together — a company event for the whole Mobile SDK team. We meet, discuss problems shoulder to shoulder, and find ways to solve them. And bond, of course, by repeating old traditions and grounding new ones.

What would we be without traditions? Probably still sitting in caves, eating berries, and scared of lightning. 

Since we don’t see each other so often, it feels natural to have something that reminds us of that fun time together during the offsite. So I founded another tradition in our team — simple and obvious. We play the [Secret Santa with dice](https://www.whiteelephantrules.com/gift-exchange-dice-game/) game. There you have it all: joy, drama, envy, and a bit of anger.

Simple rules: 
- limited budget (10–15 euros)
- a single gift from each person
- must be fun or silly

![Flutter Fellows](/team.jpg)

## Silliness is the base

We don’t meet during Christmas. It often happens 1–3 months after it. But that doesn’t stop us from doing it. In fact, having Secret Santa in February sounds exactly in the spirit of the tradition — being a bit silly 🤪

I won’t describe all the gifts we had during the last game, but one of them was a `Bullshit Button`.

![Bullshit Button](/button.jpg)

It’s a very simple device with a single purpose. When you hit the button, it plays a random sound clip with the word `“bullshit”`. That’s it.

Purpose? To express yourself during meetings with your fellows when you hear some nonsense or `“very”` important information.

It wouldn’t be true if I said that everyone wanted to win this button during the game. But there were some battles for it, for sure 😈

## Time takes it all

Many rains have passed since that game. We’ve had some Flutter Fellow calls where we asked Florian (who won the button) to use his prize for its intended purpose. But to be honest, it doesn’t sound or look that effective during video meetings 😐

So eventually, we stopped requesting to use the button.

## Tiny tiny lightning

Last Wednesday, at the end of the day, after some calls, I had a flashback about this button and its purpose...

I quickly checked the internet and was surprised by the results.

> There was still no Chrome (or Mozilla) extension for Google Meet calls with the Bullshit Button.

That sounded unforgivably wrong to me.

So I did what I must: vibe-coded a new extension 😅

## Very Important extension

So what does it do in a nutshell? 

- Adds an additional floating button with the same name on it
- Once you click on it, you’ll hear the same noise

That was it 😂

Or not? 🤔

Well, it started like that. But the experience wasn’t complete. It doesn’t make sense to express to yourself what you already know. The point is to share this experience with others.

So the next step was to add some magic, which allows you to `notify` other call members with the same extension about your honest feelings. Once the button is clicked, all members will hear the same noise.

Now the experience is complete!

## Simple. Solidary. Silly.

No more mercy for nonsense during the two hours of `“5-minute”` calls.

![The kid with fist](/kid_with_fist.jpg)

The only downside is that if you use this button too often, it will lose its appeal and relevance. Your teammates might delete the extension... and all the fun will be gone. FOREVER!

So ...

> Use responsibly! This extension is for entertainment purposes and expressing shared meeting fatigue. Please don’t use it to actually disrupt important discussions (even if they are full of corporate speak).
{: .prompt-warning }

## Epilogue

Our life is too serious. Sometimes we need to do not only smart things that will make this world a better place to live in (sure 😅), but we also need something fun and stupid. 

Sad to admit, but after all, we’re still quite primitive monkeys who smile when someone slips on a banana.

So why should we deny the silliness inside us?  
No reason.
