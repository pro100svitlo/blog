## Wrong expectations

Running is perhaps the most ancient activity, yet the question remains: why the hell do we do it? Everyone has their reasons. After so many years I am (finaly) starting to find mine.

I started to run so many times. And was always failing. Until recently.

Hollywood (and `co`) for decades were showing us a picture, where people (almost) always runs with the music:

![Runner](/runner_with_music.jpg) 

So I was trying to copy that.

But for me it did't work. At all. If I knew the song, I’d get bored fast and skip it. If I didn't know it, I’d hit `next` after ten seconds of trial. Even worse, if a track had a strong rhythm, my pace would slave to the BPM (bits per minute) instead of sticking to my training plan. 

There were times I was trying to run without any music. But it also didn work, however for another reason - I was overthingk. While running I was trying to pay attention on how I put my legs, how I move my arms, how do I breathe (this was the hardest moments), which pace I run with. The goal was great though - adapt those and perform better. It is clear that this is a difficult and exhausting process. After such sessions I did not feel good at all - was tired physically and down mentally. 


## When it finally clicked

At some moment I switched to audiobooks and podcasts: busy mind, busy body, happy me. It allowed me to `let it go`: my body was doing the job it needs to do, while I was focused on the story. 
This approach was a game changer for me.

Recently, when my body has finally `learned` how to run well enough to maintain a pace on `autopilot`, without me controlling and overcomplicating it, I’ve started again to experiment back with runing in total silence. It was the state (of peace) I was lookng for. 

No headphones, no input information that can keep brain busy (disturbed). In Ukraine we have a saying:
>  If the hay doesn't go to the cow, than the cow goes to the hay.

If nothing triggres the brain from outside, it start triggering it self from the inside.
And that's when the `mind game` begins.


## Doing nothing

It’s phisicallty not an easy task to keep brain activity low. Buddists spend years learning how to do it properly. 

![Doing nothing](/doing_nothing.jpg) 

I am not even close to achieving that level of mastery. My brain is always busy. Unfortunately not always with the tasks I want it to do:
 - analyzing who said what 4,5 years ago
 - remembering scene from a movie I even did not like 
 - crafting alternative realities based on event that happened when I was in the 5th grade. 
 
 But nowadays, when from time to time, I run without intentional brain distractors (like music or audiobooks), a kind of `neuro-magic` happens.

Instead of traditional, linear thinking, brain begins to circle a problem like a hungry eagle. It observes from a distance, closes in, then steps back for the biger picture from the different perspective. This non-linear thinking approach is strange. One must sacrifice precise focus, but instead gain a broader vision, start to see how things relate to each other in ways one could not see in the ordinary conditions.

Many people are familiar with the `shower epiphany` — that moment a solution hits you while you're walking the dog, picking up the kids, cleaning the house (put your choise here). It’s a thing that happens while you doing some routine, automatically, without much thingking. It is some background process - it is not urgent, but it's there, running.

Same happens sometimes for me during the the `long runs`: I let my brain float on these waves of `doing nothing`. I don't block or force anything. It's total freedom. Suddenly, `idea mushrooms` start popping up here and there. Sometimes it can be only one idea. Sometimes I feel my brain jumps between topics like a multi-threaded processor, doing some tasks bit by bit instead of handling one problem completely. In this state, when a valuable idea pops, I immediately record a voice memo, so I can handle it properly later.

## The Drafting

Quite often the hardest thing is to start a new task. Specially if you do not have much experience with it. In this case, it may help to be cunning, looking for weak spots and loopholes, so enter a new topic can be easier. 
Just knowing that there is a bit eaier way - helps (me) a lot. Its a blessed feeling, that you do not start from scratch, that the process is already ongoing and you just need to continue it.

I’ve started using these running as a loophole, to enter important topic easier. In other words - create a draft, which later just use as execution plan. For example, this post - I `wrote` around 70% of it entirely in my head while jogging. Not exactly same words, but the main pillars for it. 


And it is applicable to many directions in life:
- communication with wife/kids/family
- activty planning
- work/code related things
- home maintanance
- you_name_it

Anything that can be started, can be drafted first. If it can be drafted - it brings way less resistance or panic. It's no longer a fear of creation something new.

It's just `transformation` from one state to another.

## Instead of epilogue

Now, when I look back and try to analyze my experience from this perspective, I can see that its not just about running. It can be any activity that you like, that you can do without much thinking, that can fly away you: swimming, cooking, dancing, sewing, playing an instrument, whatever. 

After all, there is one thing, that really matters:
